Welcome to the Interview Guide Tool

The Interview Guide Tool will help build a customized interview guide based on the CFIR constructs that are the focus of an evaluation. Remember, the CFIR has the following structure:
Five Domains: To learn more click the Domains link on the left.
Each domain is divided into constructs and sub-constructs.
The Interview Guide Tool provides a list of questions based on actual studies and definitions for each construct or sub-construct.

Many questions provide “probes” that can be used to delve into the question more deeply. Sometimes these probes provide more insight into what the question is designed to elicit – because sometimes it is not straight-forward to elicit information outright without “leading” or biasing the responses.

Instructions for use
  1. Click on a Domain that contains constructs being evaluated.
  2. A list of constructs will appear. Click on a construct included in your evaluation.
  3. You have three options:
    1. Click on “wiki” to obtain more information about the construct including definitions, qualitative coding guidelines and much more.
    2. Check the box that selects all the questions for that (sub)construct.
    3. Click on "Questions" to select questions individually.
  4. Repeat these three steps until you have reviewed all the constructs being evaluated.
  5. When you are done – Click on “Get Guide” from the menu on the left.
  6. In a new window you will see a formatted list of the questions you chose. You can “select all” on the page and then cut and paste into a Word Document.
  7. Note that question selection is cumulative. If, after using "Get Guide", you return to the original window and select more questions, these newly selected questions will be ADDED to those previously selected. If you want a completely new set, first click "Start Over".
Words to the wise
  1. These questions are offered as a starting point – there is great latitude in how questions can be worded; concepts within constructs may not be covered by the available set of questions.
  2. Questions will most likely need to be adapted to your evaluation.
  3. Questions can (and most likely should) be reordered to create an effective interview structure.
  4. In most scenarios, interviews should be semi-structured: questions may be asked in varying order based on the participant and not all questions may be asked of all participants depending on their role and other considerations.
  5. Consider prefacing these questions with broad open-ended questions to help establish rapport and to elicit grounding stories in the words of interviewees. These questions will vary depending on your scenario.
